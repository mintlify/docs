Apples are among the most widely cultivated tree fruits, with a rich history and numerous health benefits.

## Nutritional benefits

Apples provide:
- **Fiber**: Promotes digestive health and helps maintain healthy cholesterol levels
- **Vitamin C**: Supports immune function and skin health
- **Antioxidants**: Quercetin and other polyphenols that may reduce disease risk
- **Potassium**: Supports heart health and fluid balance

## Interesting facts

### Over 7,500 varieties

There are more than 7,500 varieties of apples grown worldwide, each with unique flavors, textures, and uses.

### Ancient cultivation

Apples have been cultivated for at least 4,000 years. They originated in Central Asia and spread along the Silk Road to Europe.

### Floating fruit

Apples float in water because they are 25% air, making them less dense than water.

### Tree maturity

Apple trees take 4-5 years to produce their first fruit and can live for over 100 years.

### Global production

China produces nearly half of the world's apples, followed by the United States, Turkey, and Poland.

## Popular varieties

Some widely enjoyed apple varieties include:
- **Fuji**: Sweet and crisp, great for eating fresh
- **Granny Smith**: Tart and firm, excellent for baking
- **Honeycrisp**: Exceptionally crunchy with balanced sweetness
- **Gala**: Mild and sweet, popular for snacking
- **Pink Lady**: Tangy-sweet with a distinctive pink blush

## The saying "an apple a day"

The proverb "an apple a day keeps the doctor away" originated in Wales in the 1860s. Studies suggest regular apple consumption may indeed contribute to better health outcomes.

## Uses beyond eating

Apples have multiple applications:
- **Cider production**: Both alcoholic and non-alcoholic varieties
- **Pectin source**: Natural thickening agent used in jams and jellies
- **Animal feed**: Damaged or surplus apples are used as livestock feed
- **Wood**: Apple wood is prized for smoking meats and woodworking
