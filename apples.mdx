Apples are one of the most popular and widely cultivated fruits in the world. They belong to the Rosaceae family and are known for their crisp texture and sweet-tart flavor.

## Varieties

There are thousands of apple varieties grown worldwide, each with unique characteristics:

- **Red Delicious** - Sweet and mildly flavored with a deep red skin
- **Granny Smith** - Tart and crisp with bright green skin
- **Fuji** - Sweet and juicy with a yellow-green skin with red highlights
- **Gala** - Sweet and aromatic with a thin skin
- **Honeycrisp** - Exceptionally crisp and sweet-tart balance

## Nutritional benefits

Apples are a nutritious fruit that provides several health benefits:

- High in fiber, particularly pectin
- Rich in vitamin C and antioxidants
- Low in calories (approximately 95 calories per medium apple)
- Contains beneficial plant compounds like quercetin

<Tip>
  Eating an apple with the skin on provides the most nutritional value, as many nutrients are concentrated in or just below the skin.
</Tip>

## Storage

To keep apples fresh:

1. Store in the refrigerator for maximum freshness
2. Keep away from other produce, as apples release ethylene gas
3. Use within 4-6 weeks for best quality

## Uses

Apples are versatile and can be enjoyed in many ways:

- Fresh as a snack
- Baked in pies and desserts
- Pressed into juice or cider
- Cooked into applesauce
- Added to salads
