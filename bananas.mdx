Bananas are one of the most consumed fruits worldwide, packed with nutrients and surprising history.

## Nutritional benefits

Bananas are rich in:
- **Potassium**: Essential for heart health and blood pressure regulation
- **Vitamin B6**: Supports brain health and mood regulation
- **Vitamin C**: Boosts immune system function
- **Fiber**: Aids digestion and promotes gut health

## Interesting facts

### Bananas are berries

Botanically speaking, bananas are classified as berries, while strawberries are not.

### Radioactive fruit

Bananas contain potassium-40, a naturally occurring radioactive isotope. However, the amount is so small it poses no health risk.

### Ancient fruit

Bananas have been cultivated for over 7,000 years, making them one of the oldest cultivated crops in human history.

### Floating fruit

Bananas float in water because they are less dense than water, containing about 75% water by weight.

### Global production

India is the world's largest producer of bananas, followed by China and the Philippines.

### Ripening process

Bananas continue to ripen after being picked. They release ethylene gas, which accelerates the ripening of nearby fruits.

## Varieties

While the Cavendish banana is the most common variety sold in stores, there are over 1,000 different varieties of bananas grown worldwide, including:
- Red bananas
- Plantains
- Lady Finger bananas
- Blue Java bananas (which taste like vanilla ice cream)

## Uses beyond eating

Banana plants have multiple uses:
- **Fiber**: Banana plant fibers can be used to make textiles and paper
- **Animal feed**: Leaves and stems serve as livestock feed
- **Cooking**: Banana leaves are used as natural, biodegradable plates in many cultures
