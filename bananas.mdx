## Fun banana facts

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Bananas are berries">
    Botanically speaking, bananas are actually berries, while strawberries are not!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="They float in water">
    Bananas are less dense than water, which means they float. Try it!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Radioactive fruit">
    Bananas contain potassium-40, making them slightly radioactive. Don't worry‚Äîit's completely harmless!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Ancient fruit">
    Bananas have been cultivated for over 7,000 years and are mentioned in ancient texts.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Banana ripeness guide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Green bananas">
    Firm and starchy. Best for cooking or if you prefer less sweetness.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Yellow bananas">
    Perfect for eating fresh. Sweet and easy to digest.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Spotted bananas">
    Extra sweet with brown spots. Ideal for smoothies and baking.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Brown bananas">
    Very soft and super sweet. Perfect for banana bread!
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Banana varieties

There are over 1,000 varieties of bananas worldwide! Here are some popular ones:

| Variety | Description | Best for |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Cavendish | Most common yellow banana | Eating fresh |
| Plantain | Larger, starchier | Cooking and frying |
| Red banana | Reddish-purple skin | Desserts |
| Lady Finger | Smaller, sweeter | Snacking |

<Warning>
Never put bananas in the refrigerator if they're not ripe yet‚Äîit stops the ripening process!
</Warning>
