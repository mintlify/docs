To host your documentation on a custom domain:
1. Add your domain in your dashboard.
2. Configure DNS settings on your domain provider.
3. Allow time for DNS to propagate and TLS certificates to be automatically provisioned.

<Info>
  Looking to set up a subpath like `example.com/docs`? See [/docs subpath](/deploy/docs-subpath).
</Info>

## Add your custom domain

1. Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page in your dashboard.
2. Enter your domain name. For example, `docs.example.com` or `www.example.com`.
3. Click **Add domain**.

## Configure your DNS

1. On your domain provider's website, navigate to your domain's DNS settings.
2. Create a new DNS record with the following values:

```text
CNAME | docs | cname.mintlify.builders
```

<Tip>
  Each domain provider has different ways to add DNS records. Refer to your domain provider's documentation for specific instructions.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Do not add or change your `CNAME` until both verification `TXT` records show as verified in your dashboard. Each appears with a green check when DNS is correct. The dashboard verifies `TXT` records before certificate provisioning can complete. Switching `CNAME` too early commonly breaks HTTPS until provisioning finishes.

  If you migrate an existing domain and want zero downtime, publish the verification `TXT` records first and wait until they show verified and TLS has pre-provisioned before pointing `CNAME` at Mintlify.

  This pre-validation flow does not work for domains proxied through Cloudflare. See [Cloudflare-proxied domains](#cloudflare-proxied-domains).
</Warning>

### Verification TXT records

After you add a custom domain, the dashboard displays two `TXT` records that you must add at your DNS provider:

```text
TXT | _acme-challenge.<your-domain> | <value shown in your dashboard>
TXT | _cf-custom-hostname.<your-domain> | <value shown in your dashboard>
```

The `_acme-challenge` record authorizes Let's Encrypt to issue a TLS certificate for your domain, and the `_cf-custom-hostname` record verifies that you control the domain.

The dashboard polls DNS in the background and marks each record with a green check once it verifies the expected value. After saving records at your DNS provider, allow a short time for propagation before status updates appear.

### DNS propagation

DNS changes typically take 1-24 hours to propagate globally, though it can take up to 48 hours in some cases. Use a tool like [DNSChecker](https://dnschecker.org) to verify your DNS configuration is correct.

Once your DNS records are active, your documentation is first accessible via HTTP. HTTPS is available after Mintlify provisions your TLS certificate.

### Automatic TLS provisioning

After you add your `TXT` records and your DNS records resolve correctly, Mintlify generates a free SSL/TLS certificate for your site using Let's Encrypt.

This typically completes within a few hours of DNS propagation, though it can take up to 24 hours in rare cases. Certificates are automatically renewed before expiration.

### CAA records

If your domain uses CAA (Certification Authority Authorization) records, you must authorize Let's Encrypt to issue certificates for your domain. Add the following CAA record to your DNS settings:

```text
0 issue "letsencrypt.org"
```

### Reserved paths

Mintlify reserves the `/.well-known/acme-challenge` path for certificate validation. You cannot redirect or rewrite this path. If you have configured redirects or rewrites for this path, certificate provisioning fails.

### Cloudflare-proxied domains

If your domain is already proxied through Cloudflare (the proxy status shows an orange cloud), the verification `TXT` records cannot show as verified before you update your `CNAME`. This happens even when the records resolve correctly with tools like `dig` or DNSChecker. Cloudflare's proxy prevents the verification from completing until traffic for the hostname routes to Mintlify.

For Cloudflare-proxied domains, follow these steps:

1. Add the verification `TXT` records at your DNS provider.
2. Update your `CNAME` record to point to `cname.mintlify.builders` without waiting for the `TXT` records to show as verified.
3. Wait for verification and TLS provisioning to complete. Your site may briefly serve an invalid certificate during provisioning.

If you need zero downtime during migration, set the `CNAME` record's proxy status to **DNS only** (gray cloud) instead. This allows the standard pre-validation flow to complete before you switch traffic.

### Provider-specific settings

<Accordion title="Cloudflare encryption mode">
  If Cloudflare is your DNS provider, you must enable the "Full (strict)" mode for the SSL/TLS encryption setting. Additionally, disable "Always Use HTTPS" in your Edge Certificates settings. Cloudflare's HTTPS redirect blocks Let's Encrypt from validating your domain during certificate provisioning.
</Accordion>

### Retry validation

If your domain is still pending validation after adding the verification `TXT` record, you can retry validation manually from your dashboard.

1. Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page in your dashboard.
2. Find your pending custom domain.
3. Click **Retry validation**.

Only retry validation after you confirm that your DNS records are correct. Repeated retries with incorrect records do not speed up validation.

## Set a canonical URL

After configuring your DNS, set a canonical URL to ensure search engines index your preferred domain. A canonical URL tells search engines which version of your documentation is the primary one. This improves SEO when your documentation is accessible from multiple URLs and prevents issues with duplicate content.

Add the `canonical` meta tag to your `docs.json`:

```json
"seo": {
    "metatags": {
        "canonical": "https://www.your-custom-domain-here.com"
    }
}
```

Replace `https://www.your-custom-domain-here.com` with your actual custom domain. For example, if your custom domain is `docs.mintlify.com`, you would use:

```json
"seo": {
    "metatags": {
        "canonical": "https://docs.mintlify.com"
    }
}
```

## Lifecycle and troubleshooting

Once your custom domain is live, most issues fall into a few recurring categories. Use this section when TLS renewal fails, when both your custom domain and default `mintlify.site` subdomain remain indexable, when you need to serve more than one docs project on a single apex domain, or when your site starts returning `Disallowed origin host`.

### Rotate the `_acme-challenge` TXT record

Mintlify uses Let's Encrypt to issue and renew TLS certificates for your custom domain. Renewal reuses the `_acme-challenge` `TXT` record you added during setup. If the record was removed, changed, or its value drifted from what your dashboard shows, renewal fails and browsers eventually surface certificate errors.

Rotate the record when:

- Your dashboard shows the `_acme-challenge` `TXT` record as unverified after previously verifying.
- You migrate the domain to a new DNS provider and copied records incompletely.
- You move a domain from one Mintlify deployment to another and the previous record's value no longer matches the value shown in the new deployment's dashboard.
- TLS provisioning has been stuck for more than 24 hours after DNS otherwise resolves correctly.

To rotate the record:

1. Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page in your dashboard and copy the current `_acme-challenge` value.
2. At your DNS provider, delete any stale `_acme-challenge.<your-domain>` `TXT` records that don't match the value shown in your dashboard. Multiple conflicting `TXT` records at the same name can prevent validation.
3. Add a `TXT` record for `_acme-challenge.<your-domain>` with the value from your dashboard.
4. Wait for propagation, then confirm the record resolves with `dig TXT _acme-challenge.<your-domain>` or [DNSChecker](https://dnschecker.org).
5. Return to the dashboard and click **Retry validation**.

<Tip>
  If your DNS provider is Cloudflare and the record is proxied, set the `_acme-challenge` `TXT` record to **DNS only**. `TXT` records are not proxied, but confirming the proxy status rules out related configuration issues.
</Tip>

### Disable or hide the default `mintlify.site` subdomain

Every deployment keeps a default `<your-subdomain>.mintlify.site` URL available. Once you configure a custom domain, both URLs serve the same content and both can be indexed by search engines, which splits SEO signal and causes duplicate results.

You cannot fully disable the default subdomain, but you can prevent it from appearing in public search results:

- **Set a canonical URL.** Configure the [`canonical` meta tag](#set-a-canonical-url) to your custom domain. Search engines then treat your custom domain as the primary URL and consolidate ranking signals there.
- **Restrict indexing to your custom domain.** If duplicate results persist, enable **Don't index project** on the [Add-ons](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/addons) page in your dashboard while you request removal of the `mintlify.site` URLs from search engines, then disable the setting once the custom domain is fully indexed. See [Disable indexing](/optimize/seo#disable-indexing) for behavior details.
- **Require authentication.** For [private deployments](/deploy/authentication-setup), authentication applies to both the custom domain and the default subdomain, so the `mintlify.site` URL is not publicly reachable.

<Note>
  The default subdomain remains active because Mintlify uses it as the origin behind your custom domain and for [reverse proxy](/deploy/reverse-proxy) configurations. Removing it would break your deployment.
</Note>

### Serve multiple docs projects under one domain

If you maintain several Mintlify projects and want them to share an apex domain (for example `example.com/product-a` and `example.com/product-b`), the supported options depend on your plan:

- **Any plan — separate subdomains.** Add each project as its own custom domain: `product-a.example.com`, `product-b.example.com`. This is the recommended pattern because each deployment gets its own certificate and canonical URL without any additional infrastructure on your side.
- **Any plan — one project with tabs or versions.** Consolidate the projects into a single deployment and use [tabs, versions, or navigation groups](/organize/navigation) to separate the content. All content then lives under one custom domain (`docs.example.com`).
- **Any plan — `/docs` subpath.** A single project can be served at `<your-domain>/docs` using the [Host at `/docs` subpath](/deploy/reverse-proxy#host-at-docs-subpath) configuration. Only one Mintlify deployment can occupy the `/docs` path on a given domain.
- **Enterprise plan — custom subpaths per project.** To serve multiple projects at arbitrary subpaths of a single apex (for example `example.com/product-a` and `example.com/product-b`), you must run your own reverse proxy in front of each deployment. This routing is only supported on [Enterprise plans](https://mintlify.com/pricing?ref=custom-domain). See [Custom subpath](/deploy/reverse-proxy#custom-subpath) for the required routes and headers.

<Warning>
  Mintlify does not natively route different subpaths of one custom domain to different deployments. Attempting to point the same custom domain at multiple projects in the dashboard fails verification, because only one deployment can own a hostname at a time.
</Warning>

### `Disallowed origin host` error

If your site returns a 502 response with the body `Disallowed origin host`, your custom domain is still routing to Mintlify's edge, but the underlying deployment's origin no longer matches what the edge is willing to serve. This typically happens after a project or domain rename.

Common causes:

- You renamed your project (subdomain) in the dashboard and the custom domain is still bound to the old deployment name.
- You removed and re-added the custom domain on a different project without deleting the previous binding.
- You migrated between deployments and the DNS `CNAME` points to `cname.mintlify.builders` but no deployment currently claims the hostname.

To resolve:

1. Navigate to the [Custom domain setup](https://app.mintlify.com/settings/deployment/custom-domain) page for the project that should own the domain.
2. Confirm the domain is listed and shows as verified. If it is missing, add it and complete DNS verification again.
3. If the domain is listed on a different project, remove it there first. A hostname can only be bound to one deployment at a time.
4. After the domain is bound to the correct project, wait a few minutes for the edge configuration to propagate, then reload.

<Tip>
  If you renamed a project recently, also check any [reverse proxy](/deploy/reverse-proxy) or [Vercel rewrite](/deploy/vercel) configuration you own. Update the target from the old `<old-subdomain>.mintlify.site` to the new `<your-subdomain>.mintlify.site`.
</Tip>
