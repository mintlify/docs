Star Wars is an epic space-opera franchise created by George Lucas. It began with the 1977 film *Star Wars*, later subtitled *Episode IV: A New Hope*, and has since grown into one of the most influential media franchises in history.

## The saga

The core "Skywalker saga" spans nine numbered films, released across three trilogies:

- **Original trilogy (1977–1983):** *A New Hope*, *The Empire Strikes Back*, *Return of the Jedi*
- **Prequel trilogy (1999–2005):** *The Phantom Menace*, *Attack of the Clones*, *Revenge of the Sith*
- **Sequel trilogy (2015–2019):** *The Force Awakens*, *The Last Jedi*, *The Rise of Skywalker*

Standalone films, animated series, and live-action shows like *The Mandalorian* and *Andor* expand the universe further.

## Key concepts

- **The Force:** A mystical energy field that binds the galaxy together. Force-sensitive individuals can wield it for good (Jedi) or evil (Sith).
- **Jedi and Sith:** Two opposing orders. The Jedi pursue peace and knowledge; the Sith pursue power through passion and the dark side.
- **The Galactic conflict:** A recurring struggle between authoritarian regimes (the Empire, the First Order) and those resisting them (the Rebel Alliance, the Resistance).

## Iconic characters

- **Luke Skywalker** — Farm boy turned Jedi who confronts the Empire.
- **Darth Vader** — Fallen Jedi and enforcer of the Empire.
- **Princess Leia Organa** — Rebel leader and diplomat.
- **Han Solo** — Smuggler turned hero of the Rebellion.
- **Yoda** — Ancient Jedi Master who trains generations of Jedi.
- **Rey** — Scavenger who rises as a new hope for the Jedi.

## Why it matters

Star Wars helped define the modern blockbuster, popularized practical and digital visual effects, and built a shared cultural vocabulary — from "May the Force be with you" to the hum of a lightsaber.
