The Avengers (2012) is the first crossover film in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, directed by Joss Whedon. It brings together heroes from prior standalone films into a single team to face a global threat.

## Plot

When Loki, Thor's adoptive brother, arrives on Earth wielding the Tesseract's power, he threatens global subjugation. Nick Fury, director of S.H.I.E.L.D., reactivates the Avengers Initiative to assemble a team capable of stopping him.

The team must overcome personal conflicts and learn to work together before Loki opens a portal above New York City, unleashing an alien army known as the Chitauri.

## Key details

- **Director:** Joss Whedon
- **Release date:** May 4, 2012
- **Runtime:** 143 minutes
- **Box office:** Over $1.5 billion worldwide
- **Distributor:** Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures

## Legacy

The Avengers proved that a shared cinematic universe could work at scale. It established the template for crossover event films and set the stage for the rest of the MCU's Infinity Saga, culminating in Avengers: Endgame (2019).
