Yoda is one of the most iconic characters in the Star Wars franchise. As a legendary Jedi Master, he trained Jedi for over 800 years and played a pivotal role in the fate of the galaxy.

## Key characteristics

### Wisdom and patience

Yoda is renowned for his profound wisdom and patience. His teachings emphasize the importance of:

- **Mindfulness**: Being present in the moment
- **Humility**: Recognizing one's limitations
- **Persistence**: Continuing despite setbacks

### Unique speech pattern

Yoda speaks in an unusual syntax, placing the object or predicate before the subject and verb:

> "Do or do not. There is no try."

> "Fear is the path to the dark side. Fear leads to anger. Anger leads to hate. Hate leads to suffering."

## Role in Star Wars

<Steps>
  <Step title="Prequel trilogy">
    Yoda serves as Grand Master of the Jedi Order, training younglings and sitting on the Jedi Council. He leads clone troopers in battle during the Clone Wars.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Order 66">
    When the Jedi are betrayed, Yoda survives and goes into exile on the planet Dagobah.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Original trilogy">
    On Dagobah, Yoda trains Luke Skywalker in the ways of the Force before becoming one with the Force at 900 years old.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sequel trilogy">
    Yoda returns as a Force ghost to guide Luke and the next generation of Jedi.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Legacy

Yoda's influence extends far beyond the Star Wars universe. He has become a cultural icon representing:

- Ancient wisdom
- The power of inner strength over physical size
- The importance of mentorship
- Patience and dedication to learning

<Note>
  Yoda was originally performed as a puppet by Frank Oz in *The Empire Strikes Back* (1980). The character later appeared in both puppet and CGI forms throughout the franchise.
</Note>
