Fish are aquatic vertebrates that live in water and breathe through gills. They are found in both freshwater and saltwater environments around the world.

## Characteristics

Fish have several key characteristics that distinguish them from other animals:

- Gills for breathing underwater
- Fins for swimming and balance
- Scales covering their body
- Cold-blooded metabolism
- Streamlined body shape

## Types of fish

There are three main groups of fish:

### Bony fish

The largest group, including species like salmon, trout, and tuna. They have skeletons made of bone.

### Cartilaginous fish

Fish with skeletons made of cartilage, such as sharks and rays.

### Jawless fish

Primitive fish like lampreys and hagfish that lack jaws.

## Habitat

Fish inhabit diverse aquatic environments:

- Oceans and seas
- Rivers and streams
- Lakes and ponds
- Coral reefs
- Deep sea trenches

## Importance

Fish play crucial roles in ecosystems and human life:

- Food source for humans and animals
- Maintain aquatic ecosystem balance
- Indicators of water quality
- Economic value through fishing and aquaculture
- Cultural and recreational significance
