Bananas are one of nature's most perfect fruits. Whether you're snacking, baking, or just curious about these yellow wonders, this guide has you covered.

## Why bananas?

Bananas are:
- Naturally sweet and delicious
- Packed with potassium
- Easy to peel
- Perfect for smoothies

## Fun banana facts

<Tip>
A banana is technically a berry, but a strawberry isn't!
</Tip>

- Bananas float in water because they're less dense than water
- The scientific name for banana is *Musa sapientum*, which means "fruit of the wise men"
- Bananas grow upside down
- There are over 1,000 varieties of bananas worldwide

## How to use bananas

### Eating fresh

Simply peel and enjoy! The best bananas are yellow with a few brown spots for maximum sweetness.

### Cooking with bananas

| Color | Best for |
|-------|----------|

## Common banana questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Are bananas good for you?">
    Yes! Bananas are rich in potassium, vitamin B6, and vitamin C. They're also a good source of fiber and natural energy.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="How do you keep bananas fresh longer?">
    Separate bananas from the bunch, wrap the stems in plastic wrap, and store at room temperature. Once ripe, you can refrigerate them (the peel will brown but the fruit stays good).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Can you freeze bananas?">
    Absolutely! Peel them first, cut into chunks, and store in an airtight container. Frozen bananas are perfect for smoothies and nice cream.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
