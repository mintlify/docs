Mintlify includes built-in AI features that help your users find answers and your team maintain content. Your existing content is the context for reading, writing, and discovery.

## What makes your documentation AI-native

### Reading

Users can chat with the [assistant](/assistant) in your documentation to get answers and links to relevant pages, grounded in your content. Embed the assistant into your own apps with the [API](/api/assistant/create-assistant-message-v2).

### Writing

The [agent](/agent) writes and maintains documentation. It opens pull requests from your prompts, existing PRs, and Slack threads. Add it to your Slack workspace so anyone on your team can update docs by chatting, or drive it from your own apps with the [API](/api/agent/v2/create-agent-job).

Use [automations](/automations/index) to run the agent on a schedule or on a repository push. Each automation is a prompt plus a trigger.

Configure tools like [Cursor](/guides/cursor), [Claude Code](/guides/claude-code), and [Devin Desktop](/guides/devin-desktop) to use the Mintlify schema, your style guide, and best practices.

### Discovering

Your site is optimized for AI tools and search engines. Pages are served as Markdown to AI agents instead of HTML, and every page is available as Markdown by appending `.md` to the URL. Use the [visibility](/components/visibility) component to tailor content per audience.

Mintlify also hosts `llms.txt` and `skill.md` for your docs. LLMs use these to answer user queries and to discover what your product can do.

<Tip>
  Teach your users how to install your `skill.md` file so they get better results when using your product with AI tools. Embed an installation prompt directly in your docs with the [`Prompt` component](/components/prompt):

  ```mdx
  <Prompt description="Install the skill for your product." actions={["copy", "cursor"]}>
  npx skills add https://your-docs-domain.com
  </Prompt>
  ```
</Tip>

Your site hosts an MCP server so users can connect your docs to their AI tools.

Search combines full-text and semantic matching, so it responds to intent rather than just keywords. When a user hits a 404, your site suggests related pages. No configuration required.
