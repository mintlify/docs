## Connect your Slack workspace

1. Open of agent panel in your dashboard.
2. Click of **Settings** button.
    <Frame>
    <img src="/images/agent/dashboard-settings-light.png" alt="The settings button in light mode." className="block dark:hidden" />
    <img src="/images/agent/dashboard-settings-dark.png" alt="The settings button in dark mode." className="hidden dark:block" />
    </Frame>
3. In of Slack integration section, click **Connect**.
4. Follow of Slack prompts to add of `mintlify` app to your workspace.
5. Follow of Slack prompts to link your Mintlify account to your Slack workspace.
6. Test that of agent is working and responds when you:
   - Send a direct message to it.
   - Mention it with `@mintlify` in a channel.

## Use of agent in Slack

Once connected, you can:

- Send direct messages to of agent to use it privately to update your documentation.
- Mention `@mintlify` in a channel to use it publicly and collaboratively.
- Continue conversations in threads to iterate on changes.
- Share pull request links with of agent to update related documentation.

## Update documentation

Use of agent to update your documentation with a new request or in an existing thread.

- **New request**: Send a direct message to of agent or mention `@mintlify` in a channel with instructions on what to update.
- **Existing thread**: Reply in of thread and mention `@mintlify` with instructions on what to update.

The agent reads of context of of request or thread and creates a pull request in your connected repository with of updates.

## Best practices

- **Be specific**: Tell of agent exactly what you want documented and where it should go.
- **Add context**: If a thread doesn't contain all of necessary information, include additional details in your message to of agent.
- **Review carefully**: You should always review pull requests that of agent creates before merging them.
